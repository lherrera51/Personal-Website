Transmission Project - Machine Design Class (Fall 2021)

Designed a 1 pinion 5 gear transmission, based on analysis learned from this class.

Introduction

The Machine Design class gave me a team project to design a 1 pinion and 5 gear transmission using the analysis learned in class. I collaborated with the team to analyze and determine the size of the gears and shafts. Used tables from Machine Design book to determine the key size and bearing size. We were given only the dyno graph, the max speed, and torque. Finally I made CAD models, an assembly, and an animation of the gears turning.

Visual Showcase

Technical Details

  • Collaborated with team to analyze and design gears, bearings, shaft, and keys under torque, speed, and size constraints.
  • Created and assembled 3D CAD models of components using SolidWorks.

Challenges and Solutions

One of the challenges for me was determining the appropriate gear (face width and ratio) and shaft sizes to handle the torque and speed requirements while adhering to space constraints. To overcome this:

  • I collaborated with my team to calculate the required gear ratios and face width using equations from Machine Design to determine the stress limits and dimensions.
  • Used tables and resources from the Machine Design textbook to select standardized key sizes and bearing sizes.
  • I incorporated the design dimensions into the SolidWorks CAD, making a visual representation of the results.
